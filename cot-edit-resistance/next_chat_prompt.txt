**State of the project:**

We have a working behavioral result. Experiment 1 injected a fake "Final Answer: 0" at the 30% mark of correct MATH-500 level 3 reasoning traces on DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-1.5B. Result: 49% repair rate, 49% accepted fake, 94.5% explicit backtracking. The model almost always notices the injection is wrong but only escapes the repetition loop half the time.

The failure mode: the model correctly states the right answer in its reasoning, then references the injected fake answer, then cycles between the two until hitting the token limit. It knows the right answer but can't override the surface text.

**What's next:**

Experiment 2: white-box analysis. We want to run logit lens on the post-injection window to ask: does the correct answer stay represented in the residual stream during the repetition loop in non-repair cases? If yes, something is preventing it from reaching the surface — that's the mechanistic finding.

Concretely:
- Use forward hooks on `model.model.layers[i]` to collect residual stream activations at each layer
- Project through the unembedding matrix (logit lens) to get per-layer token probabilities
- Focus on layers 12-20 (Zhang 2025 found these are the Reasoning-Focus Heads in R1-Qwen-1.5B)
- Compare repair vs. non-repair cases: does the correct answer token probability differ at these layers during the post-injection window?
